Why to start?

It can be especially difficult to approach teaching CS or begin incorporating them into your classroom. But the benefits can be incredibly rewarding, both for you and your students. Starting one step at a time and knowing that you don't have to be an expert right away can put you and your students on the path to creating your own programs, solving rewarding problems, and increased tech literacy in an ever-changing world.

Students can:

  • Develop computational thinking skills that help students understand and communicate about math, science, language arts, and social studies.
  • Use technology not just as consumers, but creators.
  • Gain access to a career they might be passionate about and work to bridge gaps in equitable access to computing.
